Key Findings and Recommendations

1. Geographic Concentration

Rental properties are heavily concentrated in central Minneapolis neighborhoods and near the University of Minnesota. City planning should consider the impact of this concentration on neighborhood character and affordability.

2. Out-of-State Ownership

Nearly 1 in 5 rental properties are owned by out-of-state entities. This may impact property maintenance, tenant relations, and community engagement. Consider policies to ensure adequate local property management.

3. Short-Term Rental Growth

While still a small percentage, short-term rentals are growing. Continued monitoring and appropriate regulation will be important to balance tourism benefits with housing availability.

4. License Compliance

With 3.6% of licenses delinquent, there's opportunity to improve compliance through targeted outreach and streamlined renewal processes.
